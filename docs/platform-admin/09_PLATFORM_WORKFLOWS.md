## Workflow 1 — Business Registration (LOCKED)

A new Business joins the Wegn ecosystem by completing a structured registration process that establishes its identity, country context, and active subscription before any product can be used.

**Step 1 — User selects Country**
The registering user identifies the country in which the Business operates. This selection determines the Business's localization settings, available products, supported payment methods, applicable taxes, and regulatory configuration for the entire platform relationship.

**Step 2 — User creates Business**
The user provides the Business's name and core identity. The Business is created within the selected Country and is assigned a unique identity within the Wegn Platform.

**Step 3 — User selects Subscription**
The user chooses one or more Wegn Products to subscribe to. The available offerings and their pricing reflect the Business's country configuration established in Step 1.

**Step 4 — Subscription is validated**
The platform confirms that the selected Subscription is valid for the Business's country, that the required conditions are met, and that the subscription can be activated.

**Step 5 — Business becomes Active**
Once the Subscription is validated, the Business transitions to an Active status. An Active Business is recognized by the platform as a full participant in the Wegn ecosystem.

**Step 6 — Owner User is created**
The person who completed the registration is formally established as the Owner User of the Business. The Owner holds full administrative authority over the Business and its subscriptions within the platform.

**Step 7 — Platform is ready for use**
The Business is fully onboarded. The Owner User may now access the subscribed Wegn Products, configure the Business, and invite additional Users as needed.

## Workflow 2 — Subscription Lifecycle (LOCKED)

A Subscription follows a defined lifecycle from the moment a Business selects a plan through activation, ongoing renewal, possible changes, and eventual expiry or cancellation. Platform access reflects the Subscription's status at every stage.

**Step 1 — Subscription selected**
A Business chooses a Wegn Product and a plan. The selected plan defines the terms of access, including pricing, duration, and any trial conditions applicable to the Business's country.

**Step 2 — Trial or paid plan begins**
If a trial is available and selected, the Business gains time-limited access without a payment commitment. If a paid plan is selected, the commercial relationship begins immediately. Both paths result in an active Subscription.

**Step 3 — Subscription becomes Active**
The Subscription is confirmed and the Business is granted full access to the subscribed Product according to the terms of the selected plan.

**Step 4 — Platform access is granted**
With an Active Subscription, the Business and its authorized Users may use the subscribed Wegn Product. Access is governed by the plan and remains in effect for the duration of the Subscription period.

**Step 5 — Renewal occurs**
Before the Subscription period ends, renewal is initiated. A successful renewal extends the Subscription and platform access continues without interruption. If renewal fails or is not completed, the Subscription moves toward expiry.

**Step 6 — Upgrade or downgrade may occur**
At any point during an Active Subscription, a Business may change its plan. An upgrade provides access to a higher tier of service. A downgrade reduces the plan to a lower tier. Both changes take effect according to the terms of the Subscription.

**Step 7 — Subscription expires or is cancelled**
If a Subscription is not renewed, reaches its end date, or is explicitly cancelled by the Business or the platform, it moves to an inactive state. The Business's access to the associated Wegn Product is adjusted accordingly.

**Step 8 — Platform access changes according to subscription status**
The Business's access to each Wegn Product directly reflects the current status of its corresponding Subscription. An expired or cancelled Subscription results in restricted or removed access. Reactivating a Subscription restores access according to the terms of the new plan.

## Workflow 3 — Partner Onboarding (LOCKED)

A Partner joins the Wegn ecosystem through a structured approval process that establishes their authority to represent Wegn, onboard Businesses, and earn commissions within their assigned territory.

**Step 1 — Partner applies**
An organization or individual submits an application to become an approved Wegn Partner. The application identifies the applicant, their intended territory, and their capacity to represent and support Wegn products.

**Step 2 — Application is reviewed**
The Wegn team evaluates the application against established criteria including market suitability, territorial fit, business capability, and alignment with the Wegn Partner program standards.

**Step 3 — Partner is approved**
If the application meets the required criteria, the applicant is approved as a Wegn Partner. The approved Partner is assigned a defined territory within which they are authorized to operate.

**Step 4 — Partner agreement is accepted**
The approved Partner formally accepts the terms of the Wegn Partner agreement. This agreement defines the Partner's rights, responsibilities, territory, commission structure, and obligations to Wegn and to the Businesses they support.

**Step 5 — Partner account is activated**
Following agreement acceptance, the Partner's account is activated on the platform. The Partner gains access to the tools, resources, and capabilities required to onboard and support Businesses within their territory.

**Step 6 — Partner begins onboarding Businesses**
The active Partner may now register and onboard Businesses within their assigned territory. Each Business onboarded by a Partner is associated with that Partner throughout the platform relationship.

**Step 7 — Partner receives commissions based on program rules**
As Businesses onboarded by the Partner activate subscriptions and generate revenue, the Partner earns commissions in accordance with the terms of the Partner agreement and the applicable program rules for their territory.

## Workflow 4 — Business Activation (LOCKED)

Business Activation is the process by which a newly registered Business transitions from an initial state to fully operational status on the Wegn Platform, with its Owner in full control and ready to begin daily operations.

**Step 1 — Business registration is completed**
The Business has been created on the platform with its country, identity, and basic profile established. Registration alone does not grant full operational access — activation must be completed.

**Step 2 — Required verification is completed**
Any verification required by the platform or by the Business's country configuration is carried out. This may include confirming the Business's identity, ownership, or compliance with country-specific requirements before the platform grants active status.

**Step 3 — Subscription is confirmed**
At least one active Subscription must be in place before the Business can be activated. The platform confirms that the Subscription is valid, paid or in trial, and associated with the correct Business and country.

**Step 4 — Business status changes to Active**
With registration complete, verification passed, and a confirmed Subscription in place, the Business's status is updated to Active. An Active Business is a recognized, operating participant in the Wegn ecosystem.

**Step 5 — Owner gains full platform access**
The Owner User of the newly Active Business is granted full administrative access to all capabilities provided by the Business's active Subscriptions. The Owner may configure the Business, manage settings, and begin using subscribed Wegn Products.

**Step 6 — Additional Users may be invited**
The Owner may invite staff members, managers, cashiers, or other roles to join the Business on the platform. Each invited User is assigned an appropriate role that governs their level of access within the Business.

**Step 7 — Daily operations begin**
The Business is fully activated and operational. The Owner and authorized Users may now use the subscribed Wegn Products to carry out the Business's day-to-day activities.
